Pro Tips

Walking vs. Metro

Oud-Zuid to De Pijp: 20 min walk or 5 min metro M52
Oud-Zuid to Jordaan: 25 min walk or 15 min tram
Oud-Zuid to Centrum: 20 min walk or 10 min metro

Weather

March in Amsterdam: 5-10°C, rain likely. Bring waterproof jacket, comfortable walking shoes. Layers are key.

Biking

Amsterdam is THE bike city, but watch for tram tracks and aggressive cyclists. Bike lanes are serious business — don't block them. Rent bikes if comfortable, but walking/metro is totally fine.

Avoid Tourist Traps

Skip big chain restaurants on Leidseplein and Rembrandtplein. Don't eat near Damrak/Dam Square (overpriced, low quality). Albert Cuyp Market is touristy but still worth it for street food.

Cannabis Coffeeshops

Well-Reviewed & Nearby

Important Notes

• Must be 18+ with valid ID
• Max 5 grams per person for personal use
• €100 fine for smoking in public areas (Dam Square, Red Light District, Nieuwmarkt)
• Smoking permitted inside licensed coffeeshops only
• No alcohol sold in coffeeshops

Tips for Coffeeshop Visits

For First-Timers

Start with Best Friends (any location) — most beginner-friendly. Ask staff for recommendations based on desired effects (relaxing vs. energizing). Edibles are more potent than smoking — follow dosage instructions carefully. Effects from edibles take 30-90 minutes to kick in, don't eat more while waiting.

General Advice

Space cakes taste "off" but not terrible — distinctive cannabis flavor. Don't mix with heavy alcohol consumption. Bring valid ID. Most coffeeshops close 10pm-1am. Pre-rolls available if you don't want to roll your own. Staff (budtenders) are experts — don't hesitate to ask questions.

What to Avoid

Never buy cannabis on the street. Don't smoke in public areas (parks, Dam Square, Red Light District). Skip the extremely touristy spots on Damrak if you want better prices/atmosphere.
